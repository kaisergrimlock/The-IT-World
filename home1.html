Description:

The game would be as mentioned above a trading card game. The player would play the role of a “Commander” who controls a group of individuals called “Guardians”. The “Guardians” are powerful combat specialists drawn from the history and myths of various cultures and regions. The “Guardians” would have specific combat roles defined by the game such as “Vanguard”, “Defender”, “Support”, each having their own combat specialties and are useful in different situations. A player can have a certain amount of “Guardians”. When entering “battle”, six “Guardians” could be used at any one time by the player. The player has the responsibility to place their “Guardians” in a 3x2 grid taking the Guardian’s stats and roles in mind to maximize their combat potential. Winning battles would grant the player various rewards, the most important of which is token. Tokens could be used to buy new “Guardians” to add to the player’s roster. More importantly, tokens also double as a cryptocurrency that can be bought and sold on the crypto exchange, allowing players to earn while they play thus make the game more appealing to more people.

 

Guardians themselves could be traded in the Guardian Marketplace as NFTs. As well as Guardians other in-game products could also be traded like booster items and various equipment for Guardians. By creating a dedicated blockchain-based trading platform, we could eliminate scammers and third-party trading websites in their entirety. Blockchain in its current form is too secured to be hacked or tampered with.

In order to make the game truly cross-platform, the implementation of cloud gaming is needed. The player thus would only need to buy the game and regardless of hardware, whether they are using a phone or a computer they should be able to play it without any problem. This would also reduce the hardware requirement significantly as the game is not using the player’s device’s resources and storage space. Since the gameplay is pretty simple with click and drag cross-platform would not face much problem UI/UX wise and the only thing that we really need to apply is a scalable user interface to make the game look better on different devices.

 

Technologies:

As mentioned above to maximize player base and sales, the game would need to be able to run on most mobile platforms, which means it must be coded using two program languages: Swift and C# for Apple and Kotlin and Java for Android. Since our game is going to be a 2-D trading-card-based game with comparatively limited graphics, we do not need to use powerful 3-D graphic development tools like Unity. Instead, we could use the much more resource-efficient tool Coco-2DX. Coco-2DX had been used by professional developers to make 2-D card games like Clash of Kings and is compatible with C object language and Swift which, as mentioned above, are two of the primary languages we are going to use to develop our game. For 2-D graphics and FX elements, we could draw them on dedicated drawing tools like Paint Tool Sai before importing them to Coco-2DX. For the deployment of our blockchain for the game, we could use Truffle, a program designed to work with Ethereum blockchain. Since NFT, the most important part of the game is currently only used on the Ethereum system, this tool is pretty much perfect for us. For the cloud aspect of the program, we could use tools like BitTorrent Sync and Rackspace.

Skills Required:

Although it is a bit unrealistic to make a game of this caliber alone as it takes a team of many specialized professionals to successfully develop a game, it must be said that many high selling indie games were created by a very small team of developers and sometimes even by a single very passionate developer. The skills needed for a game developer are vast. Firstly, we need to be well acquainted with the programming language needed to develop the game, in this case, Java, C#, and Swift. Secondly, we need to be able to create 2D graphics in order to make game character sprites, background images as well as UI elements. For this, some art skills are required as well as the ability to understand and utilize user-centered designs. Thirdly we need to know to create and sell NFT and utilize that knowledge in game development. And fourthly we need intimate knowledge of cloud computing and the ability to use it to store game-related databases.

Outcome

If the game is successful on release it would create a whole new gaming experience for players. Players now could turn gaming into a half-time or even full-time job as our blockchain technology would allow them to trade their in game tokens for real money. The game’s success would encourage many others to utilize blockchain in game design, after which the advantages of blockchain gaming would be so apparent that other products in the industry would have to utilize this technology or at least mimic some of its features. We would for the first time see players be able own their own in-game assets. Cheats and hacks in gaming, a legacy epidemic, would be cracked down much fiercer as account theft would be almost impossible to attempt. The decentralization nature of blockchain gaming would also be able to propel our game across the strict trade barrier owned by few major developers and would give both our game and other indie game a chance to compete against AAA titles in the industry.

Blockchain

What is blockchain?

Blockchain was developed in 1991 by a group of researchers as a method to time-stamp documents so that it’s not possible to back-date them or tamper with them. However, as blockchain had not been in the headlines in the past 10 years until today, this technology went by mostly unused. That is until the year 2009 when a mysterious online entity known as Satoshi Nakamoto created bitcoin using this same technology. Digital currency had not been unknown up until that point and many entities both private and the public had attempted to create digital currency ever since computers and the internet become useable but they mostly see no success until Bitcoin become a thing.

The crux of the problem lies with trust. If one day I create a new cryptocurrency, no one would use it because no one could trust it to no have been tampered with or that some hackers wouldn’t be able to get into the database and affect the market flow. Blockchain-based cryptocurrency is designed to go around this issue. Most normal databases like SQL, usually have someone in charge of it who would enter in entries to change the data while a blockchain as a decentralized database, as such no one is in charge of the database and no single entity could change it the data from within the system. No extra money could be flushed into the economy and no money could be pulled out with a single click of a button from the person in charge. The people who use Bitcoins run the Bitcoin economy giving them more trust in the system and allowing more people to pay or invest in it. More importantly, however, is that since a blockchain database as mentioned above is effectively immutable it is therefore impossible to fake, hack or double-spend the Bitcoins you have, ensuring the people that use this money that it is a limited resource with some value in it (just like real money).

Cloud Computing

What does it do?

Cloud computing is a delivery on demand computing service – including servers, storage, database, networking, software, analytics and intelligence – over the internet (the cloud) to offer faster, more flexible resources. The first thing we need to look at is the cloud itself. The cloud is a term used to describe servers that have access over the internet and the databases and applications that run on those servers. Cloud servers are located in data centers over the world, some have been sent into space as satellite the orbits the earth. By using cloud computing, users and companies don’t have to manage physical servers themselves or run applications on their own machines. The clouds allow users and companies to access files and applications on almost any devices from practically anywhere with internet connection. This is due to a technology called “virtualization”. Virtualization allows simulation of “virtual machine” that behaves like a physical computer with its own hardware and systems but virtual only. Multiple virtual machines can be running simultaneously on a device, thus make much more efficient use of the hardware system. Because of this, cloud providers can offer their services to many more customers at once than they would otherwise with a more traditional system, and they could do so more efficiently which means less cost. Users could thus connect to their cloud providers via interconnected networks no matter what device they are on as the files and applications that they are using are being sandboxed in a virtual machine and is not normally affected by the designs and specs of the user’s own device’s hardware.

What is the likely impact?

Cloud computing take advantage of economy of scale effect since you typically only pay for the cloud services you are using. This allows you to run your infrastructure more efficiently which in turn lower your operating cost and scale as your business changes, growing or shrinking, as time goes on. Since cloud uses virtualization technology it could help create virtual workplaces whereby applications and services could be hosted by a data center and could be accessed all over the world where the internet it available. Thus cloud computing could increase the ease of working as employees would no longer need to be present at their work station to be able to complete their jobs and more people should be able to work at home thus giving people more time to spend on their families and hobbies.

Allowing students from everywhere to attend classes no matter where they are. Moreover, cloud technology allows students to access technology and resources available at the university through a click of a button. This is becoming ever increasingly useful as the COVID-19 pandemic had forced many to stay at home limited international trade and travels. Cloud technology have also revolutionized the way we view entertainment, movies no longer needed to be downloaded onto our devices to be watched and could instead be streamed through cloud-based apps like Netflix, Amazon and Hulu. This could also extend to gaming as Google announced Stadia, a cloud-based gaming application that allows players to play games without even having to download the files onto their computers (as the game are streamed to the computer screen through the cloud like movies).

How will Cloud Computing affect you?

Cloud computing have, is and will have a profound impact on the lives of everyone. Slowly but surely most online databases will migrate to the Cloud thus many services and application would thus be mostly cloud-based. We are seeing it right now with most smart phones requiring or suggesting users to upload their photos and other data onto the smartphone’s cloud accounts for storage. Doing so not only will help users store their data forever but also release crucial storage space on the phone itself. As cloud technology develops the idea of downloading software to be used directly on your computer might disappear forever as all software might be cloud-based and be sandboxed in a remote data center, with only the UI being streamed to your computer screen for you to use. Since virtualization also allows users to access databases and applications everywhere in the world, it will have a great social impact and might change social norms as profoundly as the smartphone and social media did many years ago. As applications could be accessed where internet is available, we could expect more and more people to be allowed to work at home as the need to go to the workplace grows smaller and smaller. People would also no longer need to travel for work as much while companies could hire from a much larger pool of potential employees. This could change the very fabric of society as we know it and may have profound impact on personal and workplace dynamics, though if this is going to have a positive or negative impact on our lives is still up for debate.
